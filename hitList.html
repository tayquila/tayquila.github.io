1

Call Me If You Get Lost

Tyler, The Creator

Review

Sir Baudelaire, Sir Baudelaire! Tyler, the Creator often adopts different personas for his albums, and from the ones I’ve seen, Tyler Baudelaire is by far my favorite. Call Me If You Get Lost carries that classic Tyler sound, where he knows exactly who he is and isn’t afraid to show it.

Listening to Call Me If You Get Lost feels like taking a vacation toward self-discovery. Tales of identity, love, and success make most songs on this album deeply relatable. Although every track is a hit, both lyrically and instrumentally, my favorite pick has to be “Massa.”

"Massa” tells Tyler’s full story, not just who he is, but how he became himself through his music. The song shifts instrumentally with callbacks to all his previous albums. In “Massa,” you can feel Tyler’s passion and confidence; it makes you want to root for every word he throws at you.

Overall, Call Me If You Get Lost is an immediate 10/10. Every song is a piece of art, and in my opinion, it’s one of Tyler’s greatest albums.

2

Soft Spot

JMSN

Review

A perfect mix of rock, club, hip-hop, RNB, and soul, “Soft Spot” is the title to these mix of genres! With the mix of sounds and music styles, this album makes me feel a bunch of positive emotions. Whether I desire to dance, pour my heart out, or just need a boost of motivation, Soft Spot is perfect in doing so!

This album has been on constant repeat for me, and I can safely say that JMSN has been my greatest find this year. With so many great songs after the other, I find it difficult to find a favorite but if I had to choose I’d say “Feel Like a Woman” takes the cake!

Not only can I sing it word for word but it truly makes me feel like… well… a Woman! Every word in these lyrics holds a significant meaning to me. In an age of changing identity, this song helps me to feel grounded and dignified.

“Soft Spot” is an immaculate 10/10, for an “underground” artist, JMSN certainly deserves his props for the groundbreaking work he is doing.

3

In Search Of

N.E.R.D

Review

The iconic and lively album In Search of is one of N.E.R.D’s best! It marked a groundbreaking blend of hip-hop and rap with pop and rock. Whenever I’m feeling nostalgic for the 2000s college party scene or need to get hyped, this album is my first pick. Whether it’s a high-energy anthem like “Lapdance” or a story-driven track like “Bobby James,” this album radiates pure energy.

In Search of gives a strong feeling of rebellion. It’s unapologetically weird in a way that gives you permission to be unapologetically yourself. My favorite song on the album is easily “Bobby James,” which tells the story of a young boy whose drug addiction leads to a tragic end. The song highlights the fatal consequences of addiction, especially among youth, a message that remains painfully relevant today.

Overall, In Search of is fun, energetic, and incredibly innovative. Listening to it now still feels both futuristic and nostalgic, like rediscovering something that shaped you without even realizing it. A solid 9/10 and a timeless experience.

4

Gemini Rights

Steve Lacy

Review

At first, I hesitated to give Gemini Rights a listen because of its trendy hit “Bad Habit.” However, knowing Steve Lacy’s artistry, I gave it a chance — and it was the best decision! This album shows Steve at his most vulnerable, sharing his battle between love and freedom.

The songs are catchy, playful, vulnerable, and fun! I connect deeply with “Amber” and “Buttons,” two songs about heartbreak and choosing yourself over difficult love. This album taught me a powerful lesson about letting go — the world moves on, love returns, but you must know yourself first.

My favorite song is “Buttons.” It captures falling in love, the confusion it brings, and the vulnerability that comes with it. Despite being treated poorly by his lover, Steve’s love still lingers. The repeated line “About my pride” feels like Steve questioning his dignity, showing how love can lead to losing yourself.

Overall, Gemini Rights is a great album, nearly perfect from start to finish. The final track leaves me wanting more, yet that lingering desire feels intentional. Overall, I would give it an 8.5/10.
